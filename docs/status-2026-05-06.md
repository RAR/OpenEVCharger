## C. TLV protocol surface

Spec called for 12 commands + 6 events. Shipped: **28 commands + 23 events**
(RFID + lifetime + build-info + device-id + auth-config + OTA chain
+ time sync + restart + simulate-replug extensions). STATE_REPORT is at
56 B = TLV_PAYLOAD_MAX cap; future fields need a payload-cap bump or a
side-channel event.

## D. Open carve-outs

- `FAULT_DIODE_CHECK` ‚Äî enum-only, blocked on the bench 5-point negative-half cal (F2).
- `FAULT_CC_OUT_OF_RANGE` ‚Äî decoder live; detector raise gated on
  `OPENEVCHARGER_CC_DETECTOR=1` until bench cal of the OEM CC divider (F6).
- `FAULT_GFCI_SELF_TEST` ‚Äî wired (`gfci_self_test()` polarity-agnostic CAL pulse,
  `self_test_gfci_cal()` boot wrapper); raise gated behind `OPENEVCHARGER_GFCI_CAL_SELF_TEST=1`
  until bench validates the PE3-CAL‚ÜíPE2-sense path on this PCB. Live GFCI path
  bench-validated 2026-05-05.
- Relay actuate-readback gated by `OPENEVCHARGER_RELAY_ACTUATE_SELF_TEST=0` (F7).
- **Both-legs-through-one-CT topology** ‚Äî OEM routes both 240 V legs through the
  single BL0939 CT. Bench cal worked because the EVSE-tester current-pull plug
  is single-leg. Real 240 V split-phase loads (L1+L2 of equal magnitude, 180¬∞
  out of phase) may sum to ~0 in the CT ‚Äî needs re-validation against a real
  EV before the metering can be trusted in production. This is also what
  blocks F5 (M10 full-cycle session).

**Recommended next step:** HARD_OC + SOFT_OC bench validation while the
EVSE-tester current-pull plug is on the bench (drop advertised to 6 A,

Codebase is otherwise at a clean checkpoint: every spec ¬ß4 detector
either live or bench-validated (build-flag gating only the ones blocked
on bench cal), OTA fully self-service, OCPP live with correct units,
time-sync from HA, RTC bridge across non-power-cycle resets, soft
restart from HA, and CP-wake replug for stuck-EV recovery. SWD probe
is no longer a hard dependency for firmware updates.
