## C. TLV protocol surface

Spec called for 12 commands + 6 events. Shipped: **28 commands + 23 events**
(RFID + lifetime + build-info + device-id + auth-config + OTA chain
+ time sync + restart + simulate-replug extensions). STATE_REPORT is at
56 B = TLV_PAYLOAD_MAX cap; future fields need a payload-cap bump or a
side-channel event.

## D. Open carve-outs

- `FAULT_DIODE_CHECK` — enum-only, blocked on the bench 5-point
  negative-half cal (F2). **Stock V1.0.066 firmware also skips this
  check** — confirmed 2026-05-06 by binary analysis of the SWD dump
  (no diode-related strings, no injected-ADC channel config, only
  continuous DMA scan on PA4). The OEM PCB's CP read-back divider
  clamps negative-half to raw=0, so software-only recovery is
  impossible. Path forward + recommendation in
  `docs/diode-check-investigation.md`.
- `FAULT_CC_OUT_OF_RANGE` — decoder live; detector raise gated on
  `OPENEVCHARGER_CC_DETECTOR=1` until bench cal of the OEM CC divider (F6).
- ~~`FAULT_GFCI_SELF_TEST`~~ ✅ bench-validated 2026-05-06 (F3 closed).
  Three iterations: 60 ms pulse → no PE2 movement; 500 ms / 200 ms recover →
  PE2 latched 213 ms after we gave up; 500 ms / 1000 ms recover → clean PASS.
  Bench unit's GFCI module is unusually slow (~370 ms latch latency post CAL
  release, ~270 ms hold). `OPENEVCHARGER_GFCI_CAL_SELF_TEST=1` is now default
  in CMakeLists, so the boot path runs the test on every reboot. Live GFCI
  path bench-validated 2026-05-05.
- Relay actuate-readback **removed 2026-05-06** (F7 closed as N/A on this PCB).
  Bench-tested both with and without load: `relay_main_sense_closed()` never
  asserts because PB12 is the UL2231 force-open latch (driving PB12 HIGH while
  PE12 HIGH forces the contactor open) — there is no closed-feedback sense
  pin on this hardware revision. Runtime BL0939 IA-based WELD/STUCK_OPEN
  detectors cover the same fault modes during real charging sessions. A
  daughterboard adding a CT or coil-state sense at v1.1 would let this come
  back if needed; spec § 4.1.4 step 4 is otherwise unenforceable here.
- **Single-leg CT + V-channel scale** — corrected 2026-05-06 evening: the OEM
  routes only one of the two 240 V legs through the BL0939 CT (initial
  "both-legs" reading was wrong; board re-inspection shows a single leg through
  the clamp). For a split-phase EV load, current is the same magnitude in both
  legs in series, so single-leg I IS the actual charging current — no
  cancellation concern. Open question: what does the BL0939 V channel sense on
  this PCB? F1 cal was against a single-leg 120 V tester; if the V divider is
  scaled for L-N (~120 V) the real-EV V reading will still be ~120 V on a 240 V
  charge, and reported power will be half of actual (need 2× multiplier or
  rescale of v_uv_per_raw). If the divider is scaled for L-L (240 V), power
  reads correctly. F5 (real-EV session) is the empirical answer — compare
  reported W to the EV's onboard charger display.

F10 PE continuity sense) are smaller-scope characterizations that
can land incrementally post-v1.0.0 without holding ship. F3 GFCI
CAL self-test was bench-validated 2026-05-06 (build flag default-on
in CMakeLists). F7 relay actuate-readback was closed 2026-05-06 as
N/A on this hardware revision (no closed-feedback sense pin; runtime
BL0939 detectors substitute).

Codebase is otherwise at a clean checkpoint: every spec §4 detector
either live or bench-validated (build-flag gating only the ones blocked
on bench cal), OTA fully self-service, OCPP live with correct units,
time-sync from HA, RTC bridge across non-power-cycle resets, soft
restart from HA, and CP-wake replug for stuck-EV recovery. SWD probe
is no longer a hard dependency for firmware updates.
